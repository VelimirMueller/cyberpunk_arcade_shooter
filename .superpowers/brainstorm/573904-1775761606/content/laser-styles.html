Laser Beam Visual Style

How should the laser look and feel? Pick the direction that excites you most.

CHARGING

A. Clean Energy Beam

Tight, focused beam with soft outer glow. Charge-up shows as a growing orb at the barrel. Elegant and readable. Think Mega Man charged shot meets tron laser.

Pros

  • Clear visual feedback
  • Doesn't clutter the screen
  • Easier to aim

Cons

  • Less "spectacular"
  • Simpler particle work

B. Particle Storm Beam

Wide beam with orbiting charge particles that spiral inward during charge-up, then explode outward as the beam fires. Particles stream along the beam edges. Maximum spectacle.

Pros

  • Visually spectacular
  • Great charge-up anticipation
  • Feels powerful

Cons

  • More entities to manage
  • Could obscure gameplay

C. Cyberpunk Dual-Layer

Thin core beam inside a translucent outer shell with electric arcs crackling between them. Charge-up shows as concentric rings collapsing. Fits the cyberpunk aesthetic perfectly.

Pros

  • Matches game's cyberpunk theme
  • Visually distinct layers
  • Electric arcs add energy

Cons

  • More complex rendering
  • Needs careful tuning

Charge-Up Sequence (all styles)

Regardless of beam style, the charge-up follows this timeline:

0.0s PICKUP — particles begin converging on player from all directions
0.0-0.8s CHARGE — energy orb grows at barrel, particles orbit faster, screen vibrates subtly
0.8s FLASH — brief white flash, screen shake, beam fires instantly to full length
0.8-6.8s ACTIVE — beam sustained, particles stream along edges, HDR bloom pulses
6.0-6.8s FADE — beam flickers, narrows, particles disperse, then gone
